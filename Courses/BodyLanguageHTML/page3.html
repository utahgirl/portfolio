Understand Others

50% of what people communicate is non-verbal. If we only hear the words that are said, we are missing much of what is being communicated. On the other hand, if we can discern what someone is saying through their body language, we can better understand those with whom we communicate.

Know Yourself

When you know some of the basics of body language, you can better understand yourself. Noticing your body language can teach you how you are really feeling in a given situation. Additionally, you can become aware of the messages you are sending to others without your saying a word.

Communicate Deliberately

When you know some of the basics of body language you can more deliberately convey the message you intend to send in all types of communications--professional and personal.

"The most important thing in communication is hearing what isn't said."
