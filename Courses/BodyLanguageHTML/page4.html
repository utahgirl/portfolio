HEAD

Nodding is a positive message and usually indicates the listener is interested and paying attention. Excessive nodding can imply that the listener has lost interest but doesn't want to be rude. A lowered head can signal a lack of confidence.

EYES

Looking directly into another person's eyes can indicate self-confidence. Staring, however, can indicate aggression. Eye contact is normally broken if someone feels insulted. Lowering of eyes can convey guilt, submission, or fear. A quick glance sideways can indicate minor irritation during a conversation. Looking directly up can indicate the person is thinking. Wide eyes can indicate greater interest.

UPPER BODY

Pushing back the shoulders can demonstrate power and courage. Open arms means someone is approachable and comfortable. Folded arms show a baricade and can indicate dissatisfaction.

LOWER BODY

Hands on the hips can show eagerness and readiness. Sometimes it can be a sign of aggresion. A wide stance where the feet are positioned far apart can signify power and dominance.

“A good stance and posture reflect a proper state of mind.”

- Morihei Ueshiba
